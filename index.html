World Earth Day Theme 2024

The World Earth Day theme for 2024 is “Planet vs. Plastics”.

This powerful theme focuses on raising awareness about the dire consequences of plastic pollution on our planet and its inhabitants. It aims to galvanize a global movement to drastically reduce plastic use and production.

EARTHDAY.ORG, the official organizers of Earth Day, are pushing for a 60% reduction in the production of all plastics by 2040, with the ultimate goal of achieving a plastic-free future. They encourage individuals, businesses, and governments to take concrete actions like:

  • Eliminating single-use plastics: Say no to plastic bags, straws, utensils, and water bottles.
  • Embracing reusable alternatives: Invest in reusable shopping bags, coffee mugs, food containers, and water bottles.
  • Recycling and composting responsibly: Ensure proper recycling of acceptable plastics and composting of food scraps to minimize waste reaching landfills.
  • Supporting sustainable businesses: Opt for companies committed to reducing their plastic footprint and utilizing recycled materials.
  • Advocating for policy change: Contact your elected officials and urge them to enact policies tackling plastic pollution.

International Mother Earth Day presents a golden opportunity to get involved in environmental activism and make a tangible difference for our planet. Participate in local events, organize clean-up drives, educate your community, and contribute to making “Planet vs. Plastics” a reality.






History of World Earth Day

The General Assembly established 22 April as International Mother Earth Day in 2009 through a resolution. The roots of this observance trace back to the 1970s when environmental protection wasn’t yet a priority on national political agendas.

The UN Conference on the Human Environment in 1972 in Stockholm marked the beginning of global awareness regarding the interdependence between people, other living species, and our planet. It also led to the establishment of World Environment Day on 5 June and the UN Environment Programme.

In 1992, significant milestones were reached with the adoption of Agenda 21, the Rio Declaration on Environment and Development, and the Statement of Principles for the Sustainable Management of Forests at the Rio de Janeiro Earth Summit. This marked the first major conference where sustainable development was a primary focus.
